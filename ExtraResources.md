# Extra Resources & Practice Labs

If you have completed the main paths and want to keep honing your skills, here is a curated directory of channels, platforms, and tools separated by discipline. 

---

## Blue Team Resources

### YouTube Channels (DFIR & Defense)
* **[13Cubed](https://www.youtube.com/@13cubed):** The absolute best channel for deep technical dives into Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR).
* **[MyDFIR](https://www.youtube.com/@MyDFIR):** Excellent tutorials on building SOC home labs and understanding defense mechanics.
* **[BlackPerl](https://www.youtube.com/@BlackPerl):** Focuses specifically on Blue Team operations, SOC Analyst workflows, and defensive strategies.

### Practice Labs & Platforms
* **[LetsDefend.io](https://letsdefend.io/):** Simulates a real SOC environment. You receive alerts, investigate them using a virtual SIEM, and close tickets.
* **[Blue Team Labs Online (BTLO)](https://blueteamlabs.online/):** Hands-on investigations and challenges covering incident response, reverse engineering, and threat hunting.
* **[CyberDefenders](https://cyberdefenders.org/):** Great platform offering "Blueprints" to practice memory forensics and network traffic analysis.

### Tools & Threat Intel
* **[Any.Run](https://any.run/):** An interactive online malware sandbox. You can see how real viruses behave in real-time without infecting your own machine.
* **[VirusTotal](https://www.virustotal.com/):** The standard for analyzing suspicious files, domains, IPs, and URLs.
* **[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/):** Mandatory daily reading to stay updated on the latest ransomware, breaches, and defensive news.

## Red Team Resources

### YouTube Channels (Offense & Hacking)
* **[IppSec](https://www.youtube.com/@ippsec):** The undisputed king of HackTheBox walkthroughs. Watching him is mandatory to understand the methodology of attacking machines.
* **[John Hammond](https://www.youtube.com/@_JohnHammond):** Covers a wide range of CTF (Capture The Flag) challenges, malware analysis, and offensive techniques.
* **[The Cyber Mentor](https://www.youtube.com/@_TheCyberMentor):** Great full-length courses and practical advice for penetration testing and ethical hacking.
* **[LiveOverflow](https://www.youtube.com/@LiveOverflow):** For advanced users. Focuses on reverse engineering, binary exploitation, and deep software vulnerabilities.

### Practice Labs & Platforms
* **[OffSec Proving Grounds](https://www.offsec.com/labs/individual/):** Created by the makers of the OSCP certification. Excellent for practicing realistic, standalone vulnerable machines.
* **[VulnHub](https://www.vulnhub.com/):** A massive directory of vulnerable virtual machines you can download and run locally in VirtualBox/VMware to practice hacking offline.
* **[PortSwigger Web Security Academy](https://portswigger.net/web-security):** Created by the makers of Burp Suite. The absolute best (and free) platform to learn Web Application Penetration Testing.

### Tools & References
* **[HackTricks](https://book.hacktricks.xyz/):** The ultimate encyclopedia for pentesting. If you find a specific open port or service, this site tells you how to exploit it.
* **[GTFOBins](https://gtfobins.github.io/):** A curated list of Unix binaries that can be used to bypass local security restrictions and escalate privileges.
* **[Exploit-DB](https://www.exploit-db.com/):** An official archive of public exploits and vulnerable software maintained by OffSec.

## Contributing

This repository is an open-source project. The cybersecurity landscape changes rapidly, and new high-quality learning materials are released constantly. 

If you know of an essential, high-quality resource (a channel, a platform, or a guide) that helped you and is missing from this repository, you are highly encouraged to contribute.

**How to contribute:**
1. Fork this repository.
2. Add your resource to the appropriate section (please ensure it fits the criteria of being genuinely useful and not just filler).
3. Submit a Pull Request with a brief explanation of why the resource is valuable.

Let's build the best starting point for the next generation of cybersecurity professionals together.
