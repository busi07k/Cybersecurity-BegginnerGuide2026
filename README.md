$$\color{purple}{\Huge\text{What is cybersecurity?}}$$

**Cybersecurity is the practice of defending devices, networks, data, or other sensible content from being accessed or stolen from unauthorized or criminal users. It combines various techniques to succesfully ensure that our digital data and sensitive information is safe. Cybersecurity is divided into three paths.**


🔵 **Blue Team**
🔴 **Red Team**
🟣 **Purple Team**


**I'll explain the basics of each path, It is important to know that each team is made up of several roles, each of which generally requires a different specialization.**



🔵 **Blue Team:** The digital defenders. While movies focus on the attackers, the Blue Team is the actual shield of the company. Their job is to monitor networks, analyze logs, configure firewalls, and respond to cyber incidents. Reality check: This is where 90% of the entry-level jobs are. If you are starting, this is your trench. Blue team usually works in an specialized environment, known as Security Operations Center or SOC. I'll explain more about this field into the [BlueTeam file](./BlueTeam)




🔴 **Red Team:** The ethical attackers. Everyone wants to be here, but let's be real: this is not an entry-level job. Their role is to simulate real-world cyberattacks to find vulnerabilities before the actual bad guys do. Reality check: You cannot hack a system if you don't understand how it's built. You need a solid foundation in IT, networking, and defense before you can break things. We will talk more about this role into the [RedTeam File](./RedTeam)




🟣 **Purple Team:** The ultimate collaboration. Security fails when attackers and defenders don't talk to each other. The Purple Team methodology ensures that the offensive tactics (Red) directly improve the defensive strategies (Blue). It's the most effective way to maximize a company's security posture. Many people can be specialized in both teams, which is incredibly difficult and very commendable.
