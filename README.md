## What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending devices, networks, data, or other **sensitive** content from being accessed or stolen by unauthorized or criminal users. It combines various techniques to successfully ensure that our digital data and sensitive information are safe. 
Cybersecurity is divided into three paths:

🔵 Blue Team 🔴 Red Team 🟣 Purple Team

I'll explain the basics of each path. It is important to know that each team is made up of several roles, each of which generally requires a different specialization.

🔵 **Blue Team:** The digital defenders. While movies focus on the attackers, the Blue Team is the actual shield of the company. Their job is to monitor networks, analyze logs, configure firewalls, and respond to cyber incidents. Reality check: This is where 90% of the entry-level jobs are. If you are starting, this is your trench. The Blue Team usually works in a specialized environment known as a Security Operations Center or SOC. I'll explain more about this field in the [BlueTeam file](./BlueTeam.md).

🔴 **Red Team:** The ethical attackers. Everyone wants to be here, but let's be real: this is not an entry-level job. Their role is to simulate real-world cyberattacks to find vulnerabilities before the actual bad guys do. Reality check: You cannot hack a system if you don't understand how it's built. You need a solid foundation in IT, networking, and defense before you can break things. We will talk more about this role in the [RedTeam file](./RedTeam.md).

🟣 **Purple Team:** The ultimate collaboration. Security fails when attackers and defenders don't talk to each other. The Purple Team methodology ensures that the offensive tactics (Red) directly improve the defensive strategies (Blue). It's the most effective way to maximize a company's security posture. Many people specialize in both teams, which is incredibly difficult and very commendable.

## Where should I start?

⚪ **IT Fundamentals:** You cannot defend or exploit a system if you don't know how it works. The good news is that both teams require the same IT fundamentals. This is the first file you have to visit. I created a simple path with the best free and paid resources on the internet. This must be your step[0]. Check it here: [Fundamentals](./Fundamentals.md)

After learning the IT and cybersecurity fundamentals, you have to choose a path and role. I'll be 100% honest: if you have 0 experience in IT or cybersecurity, it is very hard to land your first job directly in the Red Team. The most common entry-level roles in cybersecurity are in the Blue Team. You can't exploit something if you don't know how to defend it. After gaining some experience, it's more realistic to transition into the Red Team. However, this recommendation is only based on what I have seen; you can follow the path you like the most.
